A music discovery series following genuine curiosity into the rooms, stages, and festivals where the real music lives — told through the voices of artists, builders, and believers who show up not for credentials, but because they can't stay away.

01
The Concept

No Credentials is built on a simple and unfashionable idea: the best music stories have never been told. Not because the access doesn't exist — but because too few explorers with the right combination of taste, nerve, and genuine love of the thing has been paying attention in the right way.

Each episode lives inside a place — a festival, a venue, a city with music in its bones. An anchor artist provides the emotional and musical center. Around them, a constellation of voices builds a portrait: the people who built the thing, the ones who keep it running, the civilians who show up every year because they need to. Together they reveal what music actually means when the cameras aren't already rolling.

The tone is earned, not performed. No hype. No listicles. No algorithm chasing. Just a team who got into rooms they had no business being in — and learned that the music was always worth it.

02
The Format
Opening

The Venue Essay

Every episode opens with a written essay — the host's voice over footage of the place. No interview, no subject. Just a portrait of a room and what it holds. Atmospheric, literary, specific. Sets the world before anyone speaks.

Center

The Anchor Artist

A conversation with an artist who belongs to this place — not a celebrity sit-down, a genuine exchange. The kind of talk that happens after the show when the room has emptied and nobody's performing anymore.

Texture

The Constellation

Two to three supporting voices woven through the episode — a festival founder, a stage hand, a local who shows up every year. The people who make the thing possible or can't stay away from it.

Aesthetic

Guerrilla Semi-Polished

Shot on serious gear, edited with intention, never sanitized. The texture of the place stays in the frame. Over-production is the enemy of authenticity — and authenticity is the whole point.

03
Pilot Episode
E1
Jazz Fest — The Faithful
New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival — April/May 2026
New Orleans, LA
Est. 1970

The essay opens on New Orleans at dawn before the gates. The city, the mud, the smell of something being cooked somewhere. Then the crowd arrives and the music starts and you realize this isn't a festival — it's a congregation. People don't come to Jazz Fest. They return to it.

Marc Broussard is the anchor — a Louisiana son whose sound is inseparable from the culture that produced this festival. His conversation explores where that sound comes from, what it costs to carry it, and what Jazz Fest means to someone who grew up in its shadow. Woven around him: the festival's head of partnerships on what it takes to protect something sacred in a commercial world, a local comedian who volunteers every year because he needs to be in the room, and the stage crew who make it happen invisibly.

The music documentary space is dominated by legacy retrospectives and celebrity profiles. Nobody is making this show — the one that follows genuine curiosity into the living, breathing world of music as it exists right now, in the rooms that built it and the rooms still standing.

No Credentials sits at the intersection of music discovery, travel and culture documentary, and experiential storytelling. The IP is ownable. The format is repeatable. The relationships that make access possible are already in place.
